Research

I am currently a part of Dr Karl Lieberherr's research team at Northeastern, working on Deep Reinforcement Learning algorithms, such as Neural Monte Carlo Tree Search and its various applications, especially on combinatorial optimization problems. My research work included a fine grained evaluation of the Neural Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithm developed by Google's Deep Mind, popularly known as AlphaGo Zero. The research involved converting a combinatorial optimization problem into a Zermelo two-player Game, and training Neural MCTS "learn" the game through self-play. A fine grained evauation of the Neural MCTS is also a significant part of my research. The combinatorial optimization problem used for this purpose is the Highest Safe Rung (HSR) problem. HSR belongs to a set of combinatorial optimization games for which the ground-truth is known, meaning that for each move by the proponent and opponent, we can evaluate if the move was good or bad (as opposed to games like Chess or Go, where one does not know whether a move is bad or good unless it leads to a win or loss, rspectively).
